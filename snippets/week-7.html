  • Midterm Edit

    Based on feedback from your classmates, refine your Harmonic Collection as much as you can. Be prepared to present your collection to me next week during our one-on-one. During our meeting, we will discuss how your collection is shaping up, review your code, and answer any technical, design, and/or conceptual questions you have.

    Additionally, write a paragraph that explains your collection’s theme so far, along with three avenues you can explore to push your collection for the remaining weeks of the semester. For instance, do you have an idea for how you’d like to rethink the overall pacing of your collection? Do you want to make the design more cohesive across all entries? Or completely different? How do these routes you plan to take relate to the theme of your collection?

    In the class Notion, claim a page here by adding your name to the title, and write your explanation in the document.

    To get full credit, make sure you hit all the requirements in the checklist below.

    1. I competed my write-up by 10/15 at 7 PM ET.

    2. My paragraph includes an explanation of my collection’s theme, along with three routes I could explore to push my collection.

  • Harmonic Collection: Entry #5

    Create an index.html file and style.css file in your entry-5 folder. Design and develop an entry that utilizes media queries in a creative way. For instance, does the webpage look drastically different at a certain size? Can you use media queries as a means of interactions? How do these design decisions relate to the theme of your collection?

    To get full credit, make sure you hit all the requirements in the checklist below.

    1. I submitted a link to my entry by 10/15 at 7 PM ET.

    2. I worked in the index.html and style.css file of my entry-5 folder.

    3. I used media queries in a creative way.
