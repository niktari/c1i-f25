  • Harmonic Collection: 3 Ideas

    Bring three thematic ideas for your Harmonic Collection projects and be prepared to discuss them in class. Think of these potential themes as the overarching concept you’ll explore through a series of sketches.

    Your themes should be open-ended enough to encourage a range of content, but specific enough to inspire an idea each week for twelve weeks. Some example themes include but are not limited to: your daily commute, songs you heard while walking around New York, interesting words you came across in articles this week. We’ll be starting on this project next week.

    Create a three-minute presentation of your 3 ideas using the Slides feature in Figma. Each slide should be 1920 x 1080 pixels wide. At the bare minimum, your presentation should include the following for each idea:

    1. A slide that explains your idea generally and visually.
    2. A slide that explains why you’re interested in the idea.
    3. Two to three slides that highlight some visual examples of sketches, inspiration, ideas, or other websites you like.

    You are welcome to work off this template, but be sure to duplicate the file and make it your own. Your slides should also be designed thoughtfully. After you’ve created a Figma account and verified your education status, join our class team here. Your file should be in the “[Harmonic Collection] 3 Ideas” folder. Include your name in the file name.

    Figma has plenty of helpful tutorials on YouTube. I recommend you watch some of the videos in their "Figma for beginners" playlist.

    To get full credit, make sure you hit all the requirements in the checklist below.

    1. I completed my presentation by 9/9 7 PM ET.

    2. I have a description and if helpful, reference images, for each of my three ideas in my presentation.

    3. My file is in the “[Harmonic Collection] 3 Ideas” folder within the shared class team on Figma.
