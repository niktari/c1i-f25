Assignments

  • CD Lecture Series Reflection

    For class this past week, you attended the CD Lecture Series where designer and artist Bráulio Amado gave a talk. In the class Notion, claim a page here by adding your name to the title, and write a 250-word minimum reflection on the talk. Was there anything insightful or inspiring that you took away from his talk? Was there a piece of work that he presented that stuck out to you? Is there anything from his process or practice that you’d like to incorporate into your explorations within this class or beyond?

    No need to submit a link in the Notion Submission Matrix. Just make sure that you’ve claimed a page with your name and written your reflection by next class.

    To get full credit, make sure you hit all the requirements in the checklist below.

    1. I competed my reflection by 10/1 at 7 PM ET.

    2. I wrote at least 250 words in my reflection.

  • Harmonic Collection: Homepage

    Create a style.css file in your root folder (meaning not in a subfolder and directly under your username.github.io folder). Make sure that you link to the CSS file in your root index.html file.

    Continue practicing CSS typography and layout by styling up your homepage. Design your homepage in a way that relates to your theme. Consider how you’d like to organize your entries so it’s not just in an ordered list. How else can someone visiting your collection navigate to each entry? And how does that navigation relate to the theme of your collection?

    Think about what default styles you’d like to override, and if you’re keeping them, be intentional about it. For instance, are you keeping the hyperlinks blue because it makes sense for your collection to look a bit brutalist?

    Submit a link to your homepage for completion.

    To get full credit, make sure you hit all the requirements in the checklist below.

    1. I submitted a link to my entry by 10/1 at 7 PM ET.

    2. I worked in the index.html file and style.css file of my root (username.github.io) folder.

    3. The design and organization of my entries relate to my theme in some way.
